Empathy Gap

When Your Present Self Betrays Your Future Self

The empathy gap is a cognitive bias that causes people to underestimate the influence of visceral states (hunger, thirst, sexual arousal, drug cravings, etc.) on their behavior or preferences.

Understanding the Empathy Gap

When we're in a "cold" state (not experiencing strong emotions or physical sensations), we have trouble predicting how we'll behave in a "hot" state (when experiencing those emotions or sensations).

Similarly, when we're in a hot state, we struggle to understand how we feel and make decisions when we're in a cold state.

Real-World Examples

  • Shopping while hungry leads to buying more food than needed
  • Making promises when calm that are difficult to keep when stressed
  • Underestimating pain or discomfort when planning medical procedures
  • Setting unrealistic fitness goals when motivated that become difficult to maintain

How to Counter This Bias

Being aware of the empathy gap is the first step to countering it. Here are some strategies:

  1. Practice "pre-commitment" strategies for your future self
  2. Create decision rules when in a cold state to follow during hot states
  3. Seek outside perspectives that aren't influenced by your current state
  4. Document how you feel in different states to build self-awareness

Exercise

Think about a recent decision you made in a "hot" emotional state. How might your decision have been different if you were in a "cold" state? Write down strategies you could use next time to account for this empathy gap.
